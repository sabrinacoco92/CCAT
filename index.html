What is the CCAT?

The Criteria Cognitive Aptitude Test (CCAT) measures problem-solving ability, critical thinking, and the capacity to learn new skills. It includes numerical, verbal, and logical reasoning questions, typically 50 questions in 15 minutes.

Preparation Tips

  • Practice under timed conditions to improve speed and accuracy.
  • Focus on understanding patterns, sequences, and logical relationships.
  • Review basic math operations, percentages, ratios, and fractions.
  • Enhance vocabulary and reading comprehension.
  • Stay calm and skip questions that take too long.

Useful Formulas

  • Percentage: (Part ÷ Whole) × 100
  • Speed: Distance ÷ Time
  • Average: (Sum of values) ÷ (Number of values)
  • Ratio: a:b = a ÷ b
  • Simple Interest: (Principal × Rate × Time) ÷ 100
